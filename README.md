# Cyber-Security-in-Computing 1st Sem 
This report examines the evolution of cryptography and its importance in modern cyber security, leading to the design of a new encryption method called the Shital Cipher. It begins with the history of classical ciphers such as the Caesar Cipher, compares symmetric and asymmetric encryption techniques, and explains their strengths, weaknesses, and use cases. Building on this foundation, the report introduces the Shital Cipher, which enhances the Caesar Cipher by adding multiple layers of security including reversed alphabet substitution, ASCII and binary transformation, XOR operations, and permutation techniques. These modifications make the cipher more resistant to brute force and frequency analysis attacks. The report also provides detailed algorithms, flowcharts, and test cases to demonstrate encryption and decryption processes, confirming the cipher’s effectiveness in producing unpredictable ciphertext. An evaluation section with a SWOT analysis highlights its strengths in layered security, weaknesses in computational overhead, opportunities for secure communication, and threats from quantum computing. Overall, the report shows how the Shital Cipher contributes to data confidentiality and resilience against unauthorized access, offering a practical example of cryptographic innovation in cyber security.
